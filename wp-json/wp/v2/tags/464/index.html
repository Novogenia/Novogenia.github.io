Vitamin A (Retinol)<\/h1>\r\nVitamin A is a fat-soluble vitamin. This term encompasses several substances that have similar effects. The most important is retinol, which is found almost exclusively in animal foods. Furthermore, the body can convert beta-carotene and other carotenoids found in plant foods such as kale into vitamin A and retinol, respectively, when needed.\r\n

Vitamin A: Essential for skin and vision<\/h2>\r\nVitamin A is important for the skin, mucous membranes, and vision, among other things. \"A vitamin A deficiency can initially lead to night blindness and, in severe cases, to complete blindness. To prevent this, an adequate intake of this essential nutrient is essential.
