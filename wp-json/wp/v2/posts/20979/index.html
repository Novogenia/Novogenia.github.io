Vitamin B6 deficiency at a glance<\/h2>

  • A diet-related vitamin B6 deficiency can occur, for example, in people with alcoholism or eating disorders.<\/p><\/li>

  • A deficiency is more often caused by an underlying medical condition.<\/p><\/li>

  • Typical signs of deficiency include skin rashes, increased susceptibility to infections, and numb limbs.<\/p><\/li>

  • Ongoing deficiency can lead to anaemia and neurological disorders.<\/p><\/li>

  • Usually, an existing deficiency can be corrected with an oral vitamin B6 supplement.<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

    \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

    Why does the body need vitamin B6?<\/h2>

    Vitamin B6, also known as pyridoxine, is a collective term for several compounds with the same biological effect, including pyridoxal, pyridoxol, and pyridoxamine. This water-soluble vitamin is involved in numerous bodily processes. It is essential for amino acid metabolism, hormone regulation, and the production of red blood cells.<\/p>

    Vitamin B6 also contributes to:<\/p>

    • normal nerve function,<\/p><\/li>

    • normal energy metabolism, and<\/p><\/li>

    • a healthy immune system.<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

      Is vitamin B6 good for mental health?<\/h3>

      Besides these functions, vitamin B6 is involved in the production of neurotransmitters<\/strong> like serotonin and dopamine. As such, an adequate intake also contributes to normal psychological function<\/strong>.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

      What causes a vitamin B6 deficiency?<\/h2>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t
      \n\t\t
      \n\t\t\t\t
      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

      According to the reference values from the German Nutrition Society (DGE<\/strong>), adults need between 1.4 and 1.6 milligrams of vitamin B6 per day.\u00b9 This amount can typically be obtained through a healthy diet.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

      Although the German National Nutrition Survey II (2008) found that not all adults meet the recommended intake,\u00b2 serious diet-related deficiencies are rare.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

      \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

      However, several factors can either increase the body\u2019s need for vitamin B6 or reduce its absorption, making a deficiency more likely. Risk factors include:<\/p>

      • Use of oral contraceptives (the pill),<\/p><\/li>

      • Certain medications such as antiepileptics and antidepressants,<\/p><\/li>

      • Metabolic disorders like diabetes,<\/p><\/li>

      • Liver and kidney diseases,<\/p><\/li>

      • Digestive diseases that impair nutrient absorption,<\/p><\/li>

      • Unbalanced, nutrient-poor diets, such as those common in alcoholism,<\/p><\/li>

      • Malnutrition in elderly people or due to eating disorders.<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

        Vitamin B6 deficiency during pregnancy<\/h2>

        Pregnant and breastfeeding women have an increased need<\/strong> for vitamin B6, as they must support both their own health and their baby\u2019s development. Adequate intake during pregnancy and breastfeeding is crucial to avoid growth and developmental problems.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

        How does a vitamin B6 deficiency manifest?<\/h2>

        Since vitamin B6 plays a role in many bodily processes, a deficiency can cause a range of symptoms.<\/p>

        Common signs of vitamin B6 deficiency include:<\/h5>

        • Cracks at the corners of the mouth,<\/p><\/li>

        • Inflammation of the lips and mouth,<\/p><\/li>

        • Facial rashes,<\/p><\/li>

        • Increased susceptibility to infections,<\/p><\/li>

        • Digestive problems like vomiting and diarrhoea,<\/p><\/li>

        • Anaemia (low blood count),<\/p><\/li>

        • Fatigue,<\/p><\/li>

        • Irritability,<\/p><\/li>

        • Confusion,<\/p><\/li>

        • Numb hands and feet,<\/p><\/li>

        • Loss of appetite,<\/p><\/li>

        • Headaches.<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

          \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

          Additional symptoms in young children<\/h3>

          In infants and toddlers, a vitamin B6 deficiency may also cause motor disturbances, tremors, and seizures.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

          Vitamin B6: how is deficiency treated?<\/h2>

          In most cases, an existing deficiency can be corrected effectively with a suitably dosed vitamin B6 supplement. Additional treatments may be necessary to address the underlying cause of the deficiency.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

          \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

          What are the side effects of vitamin B6?<\/h2>

          Just as a deficiency can be harmful, too much vitamin B6 can also cause problems. Overuse of high-dose vitamin B6 supplements can result in side effects, particularly neurological disorders<\/strong> over time.<\/p>

          Possible side effects of excessive vitamin B6 intake include:<\/p>

          • Sensitivity to sunlight,<\/p><\/li>

          • Signs of paralysis,<\/p><\/li>

          • Numbness in limbs,<\/p><\/li>

          • Muscle weakness,<\/p><\/li>

          • Fatigue,<\/p><\/li>

          • Dermatitis<\/a>.<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

            \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

            What is the maximum daily intake of vitamin B6?<\/h3>

            To avoid an overdose, it is important to follow the recommended maximum daily intake<\/strong> of vitamin B6.<\/p>

            • The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has set the safe upper limit for daily vitamin B6 intake at 12 milligrams.\u00b3<\/li>
            • Germany\u2019s Federal Institute for Risk Assessment (BfR) recommends a maximum of 0.9 milligrams per day from food supplements.\u2074<\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t
              \n\t\t
              \n\t\t\t\t
              \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

              How can a vitamin B6 deficiency be prevented?<\/h2>

              Healthy individuals can generally meet their vitamin B6 needs with a varied, balanced diet<\/strong>. Regular consumption of vitamin B6-rich foods is usually enough to prevent a deficiency. For individuals at higher risk, taking a B6 supplement<\/strong> may be a sensible preventative measure.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

              \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

              Which foods are rich in vitamin B6?<\/h2>

              Vitamin B6 is found in both animal and plant-based foods. Good sources of this water-soluble vitamin include meat, fish, nuts, legumes, wholegrains, and vegetables.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t
