Vitamin B6, also known as pyridoxine, plays a crucial role in metabolism. The body cannot produce this essential nutrient on its own, making an adequate intake through diet essential. A deficiency can have multiple causes and may be corrected with a vitamin B6 supplement. However, long-term excessive intake can lead to side effects, so it is important not to exceed the recommended daily limits.<\/strong><\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t

Vitamin B6 at a glance<\/span><\/span><\/h2>
  • Vitamin B6 is an umbrella term for a group of compounds with similar effects.<\/li>
  • This water-soluble vitamin is vital for energy metabolism, the nervous system, and red blood cell production.<\/li>
  • Healthy individuals can usually meet their daily needs through a balanced diet.<\/li>
  • Deficiencies are often linked to malnutrition, illness, or medication use.<\/li>
  • High doses of vitamin B6 supplements can be harmful over time and may affect nerve function.<\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

    What <\/span>is<\/span>\u00a0Vitamin B6?<\/span><\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

    Vitamin B6 is part of the B-vitamin complex<\/strong> and is essential for various physiological functions. It includes different compounds such as pyridoxol, pyridoxamine, and pyridoxal, all of which contribute to overall health.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

    \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

    Why do we need Vitamin B6?<\/span><\/span><\/h2>

    Vitamin B6 is involved in numerous metabolic processes, including:<\/p>

    • Energy metabolism<\/p><\/li>

    • Amino acid metabolism<\/p><\/li>

    • Regulation of hormone activity<\/p><\/li>

    • Reduction of tiredness and fatigue<\/p><\/li>

    • Normal function of the nervous system<\/p><\/li>

    • Formation of red blood cells<\/p><\/li>

    • Mental health<\/p><\/li>

    • Immune system support<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t

      \n\t\t\t\t\t

      The role of Vitamin\u00a0B6 in homocysteine metabolism<\/h2>

      Vitamin B6, along with vitamin B12 and other B vitamins, helps break down homocysteine, a sulphur-containing amino acid. Elevated homocysteine levels are linked to an increased risk of cardiovascular disease and thrombosis<\/a>.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

      How much Vitamin B6 do you need daily?<\/span><\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

      The recommended daily intake of vitamin B6 varies based on age and gender. The German Nutrition Society (DGE) provides the following guidelines:<\/p>

      • 1.6 mg for adult men<\/p><\/li>

      • 1.4 mg for adult women<\/p><\/li>

      • 1.5-1.6 mg for male teenagers (13-19 years)<\/p><\/li>

      • 1.4 mg for female teenagers (13-19 years)<\/p><\/li>

      • 0.6-1.2 mg for children (1-13 years)<\/p><\/li>

      • 0.1-0.3 mg for infants1<\/sup><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

        \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

        Daily requirement during pregnancy and breastfeeding<\/span><\/span><\/h3>

        Pregnant and breastfeeding women have an increased need for vitamin B6:<\/p>

        • First trimester: 1.5 mg per day<\/p><\/li>

        • Second and third trimester: 1.8 mg per day<\/p><\/li>

        • Breastfeeding women: 1.6 mg per day.<\/span><\/span>2<\/sup><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t

          Where is vitamin B6 found in food?<\/span><\/span><\/h2>

          The body cannot produce vitamin B6 on its own, so it must be obtained from the diet. Pyridoxine is found in both animal<\/strong> and plant-based foods<\/strong>. Good sources include meat, fish, whole grains, vegetables, nuts, and legumes.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

          What causes a Vitamin B6 deficiency?<\/span><\/span><\/h2>

          Most healthy people can meet their vitamin B6 requirements through a balanced diet. Dietary deficiencies are rare, yet studies show that more than 10% of adults in Germany do not get enough vitamin B6. This was shown by the results of the National Consumption Study II.<\/span>3 <\/sup><\/span><\/span><\/span>Given this, it is important to keep an eye on dietary intake<\/strong> to ensure adequate levels.<\/p>

          Regardless of diet, several factors can increase the risk of a deficiency.<\/p>

          Risk factors for deficiency include:<\/h3>

          • Alcohol abuse<\/p><\/li>

          • Malnutrition<\/p><\/li>

          • Frequent dieting<\/p><\/li>

          • Low food intake (common in older adults)<\/p><\/li>

          • Chronic digestive disorders<\/p><\/li>

          • Kidney disease<\/p><\/li>

          • Certain medications<\/p><\/li>

          • Use of oral contraceptives<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

            \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

            Symptome of Vitamin B6 deficiency<\/span><\/span><\/h3>

            A prolonged deficiency can lead to neurological issues<\/strong> and anaemia<\/strong>. Other symptoms include:<\/p>

            • Digestive problems (diarrhoea, vomiting)<\/p><\/li>

            • Facial rash<\/p><\/li>

            • Inflamed lips<\/p><\/li>

            • Cracked corners of the mouth<\/p><\/li>

            • Mouth ulcers<\/p><\/li>

            • Increased susceptibility to infections<\/p><\/li>

            • Confusion<\/p><\/li>

            • Irritability<\/p><\/li>

            • Fatigue<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

              \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

              Symptoms in infants and young children<\/span><\/span><\/h3>

              In babies and small children, a vitamin B6 deficiency may cause seizures, tremors, and movement disorders. Fortunately, increasing intake usually resolves these symptoms.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

              Can too much vitamin B6 be harmful?<\/h3>

              When obtained from food, vitamin B6 poses no risk to healthy individuals. However, high-dose supplements<\/strong> can lead to problems, especially with long-term use. A single high dose is usually not harmful, but excessive intake over time may cause side effects<\/strong>, particularly nerve damage.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

              \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

              Possible side effects of excessive intake include:<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h4>
              • Dermatitis<\/a><\/li>
              • Numbness in arms and legs<\/li>
              • Altered temperature sensitivity<\/li>
              • Increased sensitivity to sunlight<\/li>
              • Paralysis symptoms<\/li>
              • Muscle weakness<\/li>
              • Fatigue<\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t

                Vitamin B6: What is the maximum daily dose?<\/span><\/span><\/h2>

                To avoid side effects, it is essential to adhere to safe upper limits for vitamin B6 intake:<\/p>

                • The German Federal Institute for Risk Assessment (BfR) recommends a maximum of 0.9 mg<\/strong> per day from supplements.<\/span>4<\/sup><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/p><\/li>

                • The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) sets the maximum safe daily intake at 12 mg<\/strong> for adults, which is considered safe for long-term consumption.<\/span>5<\/sup><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/p><\/li>

                • These limits were revised in 2024, reducing previous recommendations significantly.<\/p><\/li><\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

                  \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

                  Are Vitamin B6 supplements necessary?<\/h2>

                  A well-balanced diet typically provides enough vitamin B6. However, supplements may be beneficial for people in high-risk groups<\/strong> or those with a diagnosed deficiency. Over-supplementation should be avoided, particularly in athletes who may be advised to take excessively high doses despite potential health risks.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t
