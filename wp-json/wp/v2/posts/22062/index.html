            What is vitamin D?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

            Vitamin D is a collective name for several fat-soluble substances with similar effects. Strictly speaking, these are not vitamins, but prohormones. This is because the hormone <\/span>calcifediol<\/span><\/b> is formed from them in the body\u00a0 .<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

            Regardless of this, a distinction is made between two types of vitamin D:<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

            1. Vitamin D2<\/span><\/b> or ergocalciferol is the form formed by plants. It must first be converted in the human body and is therefore less effective.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/b><\/li>
            2. Vitamin D3<\/a><\/span><\/b>\u00a0or cholecalciferol is the form formed by animals and humans. Since it does not have to be converted first, it is more effective than D2.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/li><\/ol>

              What does the body need vitamin D for?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

              Vitamin D has numerous important tasks in the human body. Among other things, it makes a decisive contribution to the normal maintenance of <\/span>bones and teeth<\/span><\/b> by promoting the absorption of phosphorus and calcium and regulating calcium levels in the blood.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

              Apart from that, vitamin D is also important for, for example:<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

              • normal muscle function,<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/li><\/ul>
                • the immune system and<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/li><\/ul>
                  • cell division.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/li><\/ul>

                    How does vitamin D deficiency occur?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

                    The body can only produce vitamin D if sunlight or UV-B radiation is available to it. However, this is not always the case in central and northern European countries. Especially in the winter months, <\/span>the sun is often not enough<\/span><\/b> to ensure sufficient body production of vitamin D.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                    To make matters worse, most people no longer work outdoors, but in offices or factories. As a result, they often do not get enough sun even in summer and can also develop a vitamin D deficiency more easily at this time of year.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                    Who is particularly susceptible to a deficiency?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h3>

                    In general, all people can be affected by a vitamin D deficiency. However, certain factors can significantly increase the risk.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                    • People with <\/span>dark skin types<\/span><\/b>, as the strong pigmentation allows less UV radiation to pass through<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/li><\/ul>

                                                    Vitamin D deficiency leads to rickets in children<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h3>

                                                    In infants and young children, persistent vitamin D deficiency can lead to <\/span>rickets<\/a><\/span><\/b>.<\/span><\/b> The disease is accompanied by disorders of bone mineralization and deformations of the bones, among other things. To prevent rickets, infants are usually given 10 micrograms of vitamin D per day.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    How is vitamin D deficiency diagnosed?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

                                                    To diagnose an existing deficiency, the <\/span>vitamin D status<\/span><\/b> can be determined. For this purpose, the serum concentration of 25-hydroxyvitamin D is measured. This is a precursor of vitamin D that is formed in the liver. The blood sample needed to measure vitamin D levels can be taken in a doctor’s office or taken at home with a special test kit.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    How is vitamin D deficiency treated?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

                                                    An existing deficiency can usually be treated well with <\/span>vitamin D supplements<\/span><\/b> . With the right dosage, these reliably ensure that the vitamin D level returns to normal and the deficiency symptoms subside promptly.\u00a0<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    In addition, regular <\/span>time outdoors<\/span><\/b> can\u00a0 help boost the production of vitamin D. This is true at least if there is enough sun and the vitamin D metabolism is not impaired by diseases or medications.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    Do tanning beds help against vitamin D deficiency?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h3>

                                                    Visits to a solarium promise tanned skin and a good mood even in the winter months. They are also said to promote the body’s own synthesis of vitamin D. However, tanning beds mainly work with <\/span>UV-A radiation<\/span><\/b>. However, UV-B radiation is required for the formation of vitamin D.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    Contrary to statements to the contrary, solarium visits are therefore not suitable for counteracting vitamin D deficiency. In addition, there are possible <\/span>risks to skin health<\/span><\/b>. For this reason, the German Federal Office for Radiation Protection, among others, strongly advises against the use of solariums.<\/span>3<\/sup><\/span><\/span>\u00a0<\/p>

                                                    How common is a vitamin D deficiency?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

                                                    Severe deficiency symptoms are rare. Nevertheless, many people are affected by an inadequate supply of vitamin D. According to the results of the National Consumption Study II, e.g. <\/span>82 percent of men<\/span><\/b> and <\/span>91 percent of women<\/span><\/b> in Germany do not have the recommended vitamin D intake.4<\/sup><\/span><\/span><\/span>\u00a0It is therefore often useful to check the vitamin D status even without visible symptoms.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    Can you overdose on vitamin D?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

                                                    An excess of vitamin D from excessive supplementation can lead to <\/span>increased calcium levels in the blood <\/span><\/b>\u2013 and this can cause health conditions such as nausea, vomiting and, in severe cases, kidney damage. The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) recommends a maximum daily intake of up to 100 micrograms of vitamin D for adults as safe.5<\/sup><\/span><\/span><\/span>\u00a0This corresponds to five times the daily requirement.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    What are the consequences of vitamin D excess?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h3>

                                                    The excess promotes the absorption of calcium from food and the release of the mineral from the bones. This results in an increase in the calcium level in the blood, which can manifest itself with numerous symptoms. A permanent oversupply can even lead to <\/span>kidney damage<\/span><\/b>, <\/span>vascular calcification<\/span><\/b> and an increased <\/span>risk of osteoporosis<\/span><\/b>.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    What helps with a vitamin D overdose?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h3>

                                                    In the event of an overdose of vitamin D, the <\/span>intake of dietary supplements should be stopped immediately.<\/span><\/b> In severe cases, medical attention may be needed to normalize blood calcium levels.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                                                    How can vitamin D deficiency be prevented?<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/h2>

                                                    In order to be able to produce enough vitamin D, the body needs sun. Regular <\/span>stays outdoors<\/span><\/b> therefore help to prevent a deficiency. Especially in winter, the additional intake of a <\/span>dietary supplement<\/span><\/b> can\u00a0 be useful.
