                  Can you overdose on magnesium with your diet?<\/h2>

                  Just like a deficiency, an excess of magnesium can also be harmful to your health. <\/span>Possible side effects of an overdose include diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting. <\/span>However, a magnesium overdose is virtually impossible in healthy individuals if intake is limited to a normal diet.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                  Magnesium supplements that <\/span>are too high,<\/span><\/b> on the other hand, can cause side effects such as diarrhea. To avoid this, you should not exceed the recommended maximum amount. According to the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA), the maximum recommended amount of supplemental magnesium from dietary supplements for all people aged four and over is 250 milligrams per day<\/span>.<\/span>2<\/sup><\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

                  What are magnesium-depleting factors?<\/h2>

                  Magnesium-depleting factors are those that inhibit magnesium absorption or increase the body\u2019s magnesium requirements. <\/span>As a result, they can promote the development of <\/span>magnesium deficiency<\/span><\/b>. <\/span>Common magnesium-depleting factors include regular alcohol consumption, intense physical activity, and chronic stress. <\/span>Additionally, hormonal contraceptives and some medications can negatively affect magnesium levels.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                  Other possible causes of magnesium deficiency are various diseases that can increase the body’s magnesium needs. These disease-related causes include, for example, Crohn’s disease<\/a>, hyperthyroidism<\/a><\/span>\u00a0and kidney disease.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

                  When are magnesium supplements useful?<\/h2>

                  Basically, you should cover your magnesium needs primarily through your diet. <\/span>In most cases, this works very well through the regular consumption of magnesium-rich foods. In certain situations, the additional intake of a magnesium supplement can still be useful.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>

                  A magnesium supplement can help to meet increased requirements or to compensate for an existing deficiency. Whether a magnesium supplement is necessary can be determined by <\/span>measuring magnesium levels in the blood.<\/span><\/b> The necessary blood sample can be taken both in a doctor’s office and at home with a suitable test kit.<\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t
