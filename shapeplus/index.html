Science: Today, there are already about 4 million scientific publications examining the effects of specific gene defects on the human body. A typical finding might sound like this: “If you have gene defect X, vitamin Y cannot be converted and remains ineffective.” A genetic test is only included in the program if the effects of a gene defect have been independently confirmed by at least three different studies. Therefore, the influence of a gene defect is always supported by multiple scientific studies, and the sources for these can be found at the back of each chapter. The genetic traits provided by this analysis are thus considered to be based on scientific research.

Recommendations: Recommendations based on your genetic profile. The recommendations derived from your genetic traits are often not determined by studies but are frequently logical conclusions. For example, if a certain vitamin is ineffective due to a gene variation, the conclusion might be to switch to another vitamin with a similar effect. This can be achieved through changes in your diet or supplements. It is important to understand that the recommendations developed by our experts are not based on randomized, placebo-controlled studies but are logical inferences based on your genetic traits.

Scientific Information

That genes affect body weight, the effectiveness of certain weight loss strategies, and the ability to deal with certain nutrients is supported by multiple scientific studies for each gene. Therefore, the genetic traits determined by this analysis are considered scientifically confirmed.

The adaptations of micronutrient dosing or dietary recommendations derived from these scientific findings require the so-called gold standard for clear scientific proof – these are randomised, placebo-controlled studies. These already exist for folic acid and vitamin B2 but have not yet been published for all genetic effects in this programme. For this reason, the genetic adaptation of micronutrient dosing and food evaluation should be considered logical conclusions and not scientifically proven. Your optional supplements are thus scientifically tailored to your lifestyle, your blood values, and additionally experimentally to your genes – depending on your choice
of analyses.
